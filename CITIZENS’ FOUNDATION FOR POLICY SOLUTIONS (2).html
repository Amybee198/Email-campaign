Traditional Knowledge

Documentation of Traditional Knowledge of the Naga Tribes in the hills of Manipur




A people without the knowledge of their past history, origin and culture is like a tree without roots.

-Marcus Garvey



India has a rich heritage of traditional art, medicine system, culture and biodiversity with 104 million indigenous peoples residing, therein. This heritage is encapsulated in the form of tribals’ and ethnic groups’ traditions and expertise, also known as ‘traditional knowledge’, which mostly exists in local languages and dialects and largely remains undocumented. We’re excited to kick off a series aimed at raising awareness on this important issue. Stay tuned for our upcoming events!


Karmanye Thandani

President
